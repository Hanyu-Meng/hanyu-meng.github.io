# Hanyu Meng

I am a PhD Candidate at The University of New South Wales. My research focuses on audio signal processing, human auditory modelling, spatial audio, and music information retrieval.

I am particularly interested in developing biologically inspired and interpretable audio front-ends that bridge human auditory perception and modern machine listening systems.

---

<section id="research"></section>

## Research Interests

### Human Auditory Modelling

I study biologically inspired auditory mechanisms, including adaptive front-ends, cochlear-inspired processing, dynamic range control, and robust acoustic representation learning.

### Spatial Audio and Binaural Hearing

My research explores binaural spatial cues, selective auditory attention, target speaker extraction, sound source localisation, and spatial machine listening in complex acoustic environments.

### Music Information Retrieval

I am also interested in music transcription, part-aware choral analysis, perceptually motivated audio features, and lightweight music understanding models.
