About

Building auditory-inspired intelligence for machine hearing.

I am a PhD Candidate at The University of New South Wales. My research focuses on audio signal processing, human auditory modelling, spatial audio, and music information retrieval.

I am particularly interested in designing interpretable and adaptive audio front-ends that bridge human auditory perception and modern deep learning systems.

Research

Research Interests

Human Auditory Modelling

Biologically inspired front-ends, auditory adaptation, cochlear gain control, and interpretable acoustic representations.

Spatial Audio & Binaural Hearing

Binaural spatial cues, selective auditory attention, target speaker extraction, localisation, and robust spatial machine listening.

Music Information Retrieval

Music transcription, part-aware choral analysis, perceptually motivated features, and lightweight music understanding models.
